Showdown

This is a site for all things Showdown, created and maintained by Ellie. Use the links at the top of the page to find things.

What is Showdown?

Showdown is a table-based racket sport with similarities to table tennis and air hockey. Created by Joe Lewis and Patrick York in the 1970s, it has grown in popularity throughout the world and is now played in countries around Europe, Asia, South America and Africa.

How do you play?

Showdown is played on a large, rectangular table with a goal at each end, a centre screen in the middle with a gap underneath and raised edges (side walls). Athletes wear blackout eyeshades when they play.
The aim of the game is to hit the ball across the table using a wooden bat and score into your opponent's goal, while you block them from doing the same. Players wear a protective glove on their batting hand.

A very brief overview:

  • Individual games consist of two players (one at each end), a referee and a coach for each player (optional).
  • Players get two serves each and must hit the ball on their side once before it passes under the centre screen.
  • Goals are worth 2 points and fouls are worth 1 point. You can receive points no matter whose serve it is.
  • The game ends when one player reaches a score of 11 with a 2-point lead. If there is not a 2-point lead, play carries on until a 2-point lead is achieved.
  • Matches can be best of 1, 3 or 5 sets.
  • Team games consist of 3 players on each team, who rotate after 3 serves instead of 2. A score of 31 with a 2-point lead must be reached before the game ends.
